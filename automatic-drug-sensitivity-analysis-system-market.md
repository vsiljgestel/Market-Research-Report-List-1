<p><p>The Automatic Drug Sensitivity Analysis System Market encompasses various techniques for evaluating microbial response to antibiotics. Turbidimetry/Colorimetry involves measuring changes in light transmission or color intensity in a solution, indicating bacterial growth or inhibition after drug exposure. Coulter's Principle, on the other hand, relies on detecting variations in electrical resistance as cells pass through an aperture, allowing for precise quantification of cell concentration and size. Both methods enhance the efficiency and accuracy of drug sensitivity testing in clinical settings.</p></p>
