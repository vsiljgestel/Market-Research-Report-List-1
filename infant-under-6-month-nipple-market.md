<p><p>The infant nipple market for babies under 6 months features two primary types: round hole nipples and cross cut nipples. Round hole nipples are designed for a steady flow of milk, suitable for breastfeeding and formula feeding, allowing infants to suckle comfortably. In contrast, cross cut nipples have a slit-shaped opening, enabling a variable flow, which can accommodate thicker liquids like formula or cereal. Both types cater to different feeding preferences and developmental needs, ensuring optimal nutrition for infants.</p></p>
