<p><p>The hemodynamic monitors market is segmented into three primary types: non-invasive, minimally invasive, and invasive monitors. Non-invasive monitors assess hemodynamic parameters without penetrating the skin, using techniques like ultrasound and oscillometry. Minimally invasive monitors involve minimal skin intrusion, often using catheters that provide real-time data, reducing risks compared to invasive methods. Invasive monitors entail direct measurement through catheters placed in blood vessels or the heart, offering precise data essential for critically ill patients but carrying higher risks of complications.</p></p>
