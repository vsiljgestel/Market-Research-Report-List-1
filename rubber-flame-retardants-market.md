<p><p>The rubber flame retardants market features several key types: Organic Halogen Flame Retardants, which contain bromine or chlorine and effectively inhibit flame propagation; Organic Phosphorus Flame Retardants, which utilize phosphorus compounds to promote char formation and reduce flammability; and Inorganic Flame Retardants, such as metal hydroxides, that function by releasing water vapor to cool and dilute flammable gases. Each type serves different rubber applications, balancing flame resistance with environmental and health considerations.</p></p>
