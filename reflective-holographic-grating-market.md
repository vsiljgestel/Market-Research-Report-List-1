<p><p>The reflective holographic grating market is segmented into flat and concave types. Flat holographic gratings are designed for applications requiring uniform diffraction across a flat surface, often used in telecommunications and optical devices. In contrast, concave holographic gratings curve inward, allowing for enhanced light focusing and improved imaging quality, making them suitable for spectrometry and laser systems. Each type serves distinct applications based on their optical characteristics, influencing demand within various industries, including telecommunications, defense, and scientific research.</p></p>
