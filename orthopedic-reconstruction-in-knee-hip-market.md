<p><p>The orthopedic reconstruction market for knee and hip implants features several types aimed at addressing different patient needs. Posterior Stabilized (PS) Fixed-bearing implants provide stability by preventing posterior translation of the tibia. Cruciate-retaining (CR) Fixed-bearing designs preserve the posterior cruciate ligament for natural knee function. Mobile-bearing implants allow for greater range of motion and load distribution. Unicompartimental implants target specific knee compartments. Knee revision products address failures of previous surgeries, while "Others" encompasses specialized or emerging implant options.</p></p>
